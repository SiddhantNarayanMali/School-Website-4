Library

The school library is open to all bonafide students of the school. All classes are provided with at least one library period per week. Students have to observe the following rules. Each student is issued only one book at a time for a period of one week. They must maintain absolute silence in the library. Books other than reference books are issued for one week. if the books are returned late, the borrowing facility is withdrawn for 4 weeks. Books lost, marked, disfigured or damaged will have to be replaced or paid for returned late, the borrowing facility is withdrawn for 4 weeks. Students are not allowed to bring their personal books or belongings to the library. Any child found damaging or defacing a book regularly will be debarred from the library. In case a particular book is not returned to the library, the student is required to pay a fine.

Social Responsibilities

BLOOD DONATION CAMP BY STAFF
On 07-Nov- 2017, Tuesday

Blood donation camp was conducted on 07-Nov- 2017, Tuesday at Delhi Public School, Miyapur campus. It was organized by DPS, Miyapur along with THE INDIAN RED CROSS SOCIETY. The Red Cross officials checked all the willing donors from academic and admin staff members for eligibility and took the blood from 14 persons including Principal Madam. The Society representatives sent the reports of the blood tests and Donor Cards's and the staff received appreciation letter from the honorable State Governor. Kudos to the staff for being exemplary.

CELEBRATING CHILDREN'S DAY WITH A DIFFERENCE
On 14 TH NOV 2017, Tuesday

On 14 TH NOV 2017, students from Grade VII and Grade VI had the opportunity to visit the Mother Teresa's home for Destitute and Rajeshwari Old Age Home located respectively on 14 th November 2017. The children gained a deep insight into some of the bitter and disillusioned realities of life which turned out to be an eye-opener for those who averred on their way back to be always with their parents through thick and thin.Our school believes in giving first hand experiences to the learners and to sensitize the young towards the needy who have been neglected. Students offered utility items which are required by the trusts. Students from all classes donated generously for the cause. Students presented a cultural programme to uplift the spirits of the inmates. It was really a joyous experience for all the students to visit the hospice and interact with its inmates. The most inspiring thing there was that in spite of facing so many hardships, the people here have brave hearts and are still trying to find endless ways to be happy to live. The effort of the helpers is truly commendable and restored our faith in humanity.

SWACHH BHARAT CAMPAIGN
On 29 August 2017, Tuesday

The role of schools is instrumental in realizing the Prime ministera's vision of Swachh Bharat .The students of Grades V, VI and VII of DPS Miyapur went on a rally to the nearby Bowrampet village on 29/08.17. They carried placards highlighting the importance of a clean and healthier India .That was not all, the rally culminated with a very inspirational song and a street play in the local vernacular. The student's rousing performance did strike a chord with the villagers and they shared their grievances and expectations with the students who conducted a survey on major issues on sanitation and hygiene. The data was compiled, graphically interpreted and analyzed. Learners then shared their findings with their fellow mates and the Environment Friendly Girl and Boy Prefect, Head Boy and Head Girl shared the same with the Gram Panchayat officials. This was indeed a great experiential learning and an eye opener for the learners as they had a brush with the true picture of rural life.
